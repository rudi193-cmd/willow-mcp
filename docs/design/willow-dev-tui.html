The gate in this codebase is already named sap. So the whole surface got read as a tree: the trunk is overall health, sap is the task queue in motion (or not), roots are the persistent store, rings are confirmed schema mappings, canopy is the agent fleet, and leaves are knowledge atoms — falling into litter, the receipt log, once they're acted on.

A seventh part joined once egress got its own gate: stomata, the pores that decide whether an app may exchange anything with the world outside the tree at all. consent.internet is the operator's standing switch, task_net is the capability to ever ask, and an egress lease is the time-boxed grant that actually opens one — all three, or the pore stays shut. Unlike sap, roots, and the rest, stomata aren't a mockup: willow-mcp gates renders this same three-key state today, on/off, egress-lease shaped.

Rendered dark-only, on purpose — a terminal doesn't get a light mode, and the tree metaphor only works against something like a night sky. Palette: bark #0a0a0a, sap #e2a13f, moss #7ec26a, rust #c05f42. Display face is an old-style serif used only for the wordmark and titles; every number and every table stays in monospace, because in both the TUI and its mirror, the digits are the point.
